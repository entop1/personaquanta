Methodology · Data sources

Where the numbers come from

Every index references established public datasets — not generated assumptions. This answers the first question any CFO or compliance officer will ask.

LEP — Lifetime Earning Potential
Age-Earnings Profile Model
Built on age-earnings profiles modelling income trajectory over 20–35 years. Discount coefficients applied by education level and industry sector. Result is a range — not a single number.
Sources: Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS, USA) · Office for National Statistics (ONS, UK)
NW — Net Worth Benchmarking
Cohort Wealth Distribution
Reference values from median household wealth data segmented by age cohort. Gives context: not just absolute position, but where the user stands relative to their demographic peer group.
Sources: OECD Wealth Distribution Database · Eurostat household wealth statistics
HPI — Human Potential Index
Capital Resilience Proxy
Calculated from correlation between education level, professional certification, and historical economic mobility data. Strictly a self-awareness tool — never used for credit or underwriting decisions.
Sources: Economic mobility research · Education-income correlation data
CONF — Confidence Score
Data Completeness Metric
Pure data-completeness metric. Each verified data point carries higher weight than an approximation. Higher CONF = narrower, more actionable index ranges. Transparent algorithm — no subjective judgment.
Algorithm: Input completeness · Verification level · Data consistency
